Monetization Shifts

As development costs for high-definition AAA games skyrocketed, publishers began to rely on new revenue models. The traditional $60 price tag was no longer seen as sufficient to cover massive budgets and provide ongoing support.

The Proliferation of New Models

Downloadable Content (DLC)

DLC evolved from simple cosmetic items into substantial post-launch additions, such as new story chapters, map packs for multiplayer, or new characters. This allowed companies to extend the life and revenue stream of a successful game.

Microtransactions & Loot Boxes

This model involves selling small virtual items, such as character skins, in-game currency, or "loot boxes" (randomized bundles of items). While often cosmetic, this practice became highly controversial when items offered gameplay advantages ("pay-to-win").

The Season Pass

To bundle these new models, the "Season Pass" was introduced. Players could pay an upfront fee to get access to all planned DLC for a game, often at a slight discount. This secured revenue for the publisher and guaranteed content for the player.
