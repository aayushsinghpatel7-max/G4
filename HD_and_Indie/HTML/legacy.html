Legacy of the HD/Indie Era

The seventh generation did not just introduce better graphics; it fundamentally transformed the video game industry. The changes it brought established a new foundation for modern gaming that continues to this day.

Lasting Industry Changes

High-Fidelity Graphics as Standard

Thanks to the Xbox 360 and PS3, cinematic, high-definition graphics became the expected standard for all major AAA releases, closing the gap between game visuals and blockbuster films.

The Centrality of Online Play

Online functionality ceased to be an add-on and became a core component. Digital distribution (like Xbox Live Arcade and PlayStation Network), online multiplayer, friend lists, and achievements became integral to the console experience.

The Rise of Indie Games

Digital storefronts gave small, independent developers a platform to sell their games directly to players. This "indie revolution" introduced a wave of creativity, innovation, and new genres that became a vital part of the gaming ecosystem.

Market Fragmentation

The market was no longer just a two- or three-console race. The rise of powerful PC gaming via platforms like Steam and the explosive, sudden dominance of mobile gaming (smartphones and tablets) created a more fragmented, diverse, and accessible industry than ever before.
