PlayStation 3

When the PlayStation 3 (PS3) was released in 2006, the company revealed a 20GB model priced at $499 and a premium version of 60GB for $599. This was nearly double the cost of the base Xbox360. This was because the PlayStation 3 had more advanced hardware components. Sony wanted the PS3 to be seen not just as a game console but as a powerful computer. However, the PS3 unique architecture proved to be difficult for developers to optimize. While capable of impressive performance when properly used, early PS3 games often failed to demonstrate the system's full potential. This contributed to a challenging launch period where multiplatform titles frequently performed better on the Xbox 360 despite the PS3's theoretically superior hardware.

PS3 Technology and Features

  • First console with built-in HDMI port as standard
  • First console capable of 1080p output
  • First console with integrated Blu-ray technology
  • Standard hard drive across all models
  • Integrated PlayStation Network for online gaming (free, unlike Xbox Live)

These features positioned the PlayStation 3 as the most technologically advanced console of its generation.

PS3 challenges

  • High Price Point: At $499-$599, many consumers found the console expensive, particularly compared to the Xbox 360's more affordable options.
  • Limited Launch Titles that didn't demonstrate clear advantages over Xbox 360's established library.
  • Developer Challenges: The Cell processor's complexity meant many early games failed to showcase the system's capabilities, leading to same or worse performance compared to Xbox 360 versions.
  • Supply Constraints: Manufacturing complexity and component costs limited Sony's ability to produce units rapidly
