History of Video Games

From pixelated arcade classics to sprawling modern open worlds, video games have evolved into one of the most complex and creative art forms of our time. This project explores the major eras of gaming, their cultural impact, and how technology shaped the way we play.

Timeline of Video Game History

1970s–1980s — Arcade Games

The golden age of coin-operated machines that introduced classics like Space Invaders and Pac-Man, shaping the early foundations of interactive entertainment.

1980s–1990s — 8-Bit and 16-Bit Games

The birth of console gaming — from Nintendo and Sega rivalry to iconic franchises like Mario and Sonic.

1990s — Home Consoles

The living room revolution: when home systems became powerful enough to rival arcade machines.

2000s — 3D Games

The jump into three dimensions transformed game worlds into immersive spaces, driven by PlayStation and PC innovation.

2010s — HD and Indie Games

Independent creators redefined creativity as HD graphics and digital distribution opened new frontiers for storytelling.

2020s — Modern Games

From virtual reality to AI-driven design, games continue to evolve as the ultimate intersection of technology and imagination.
