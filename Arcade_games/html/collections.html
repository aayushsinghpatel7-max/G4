Preservation & Museum Collections

Emulation as Documentation

Projects like MAME preserve hardware behavior and software for study and access. Beyond playability, accurate emulation documents how systems worked and how games felt to use. (MAME)

Playable Archives in the Browser

Collections such as the Internet Archive's Internet Arcade bring hundreds of classic titles to the public through browser-based emulation, broadening access while highlighting preservation challenges. (online-archives)

Museums & Public Memory

Institutions like The Strong's World Video Game Hall of Fame keep public awareness alive with curated selections, original cabinets, and interactive narratives that contextualize arcade history. (museums)
