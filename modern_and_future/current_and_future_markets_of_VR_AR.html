VR/AR Markets

Headsets are shrinking, apps are growing, and use‑cases are diversifying. Below is a quick map of where value is created today — and where it’s headed next.

Consumer

  • Gaming & Fitness: rhythm, racing, sims; mixed‑reality workouts at home.
  • Media: big‑screen mode, volumetric concerts, sports viewing with stats.
  • Creators: 3D sculpting, spatial video capture, virtual production stages.

Enterprise & Industry

  • Training & Simulation: aviation, defense, surgery, field repairs.
  • Design & Visualization: CAD reviews at full scale; digital twins.
  • Remote Support: AR annotations for technicians and logistics.

Education & Healthcare

  • Classrooms: interactive labs, history reconstructions, language immersion.
  • Therapy: exposure, pain management, motor rehab.
  • Clinician tools: surgical planning, AR overlays, telepresence.

What’s Next (Trends)

  1. Mixed Reality by default — passthrough + room understanding for everyday use.
  2. Better comfort — lighter optics (pancake), better batteries, more ergonomic straps.
  3. App ecosystems — spatial browsers, cross‑platform engines, UGC worlds.
  4. Safety & standards — privacy for biometrics, open avatars/items, child‑safe spaces.
