Defining the "Best" Mobile Game: An Analysis

Genshin Impact

Developed by miHoYo, Genshin Impact redefined what a free-to-play mobile game could be. It’s a massive open-world RPG with stunning graphics and deep gameplay, rivaling console titles. Genshin Impact is a monetization powerhouse. It achieved this status not just through gameplay, but through a "gacha" system that is deeply integrated into the player’s pro- gression.

  • Revenue: It is one of the fastest games to reach $4 billion in lifetime revenue (as of 2023) on mobile platforms.
  • Player Spending: It consistently earns over $1 billion annually from its player base.
  • Demographics: It shattered the "casual" mobile stereotype, attracting a dedi- cated core gamer audience with average play sessions exceeding 30 minutes.

Candy Crush Saga

If Genshin Impact represents the "core" gamer, Candy Crush Saga (King) represents total market saturation and longevity. Launched in 2012, it remains a dominant force in the "casual gaming" space. Candy Crush’s success is built on a perfectly addictive puzzle loop, social integration, and a "freemium" model that monetizes small, high-value purchases.

  • Downloads: The game has been downloaded over 3 billion times life-to-date.
  • Revenue: A decade after its launch, it still generates over $1 billion per year, proving its incredible longevity.
  • User Base: Its "best" status comes from its reach. It appeals to a demographic that traditional gaming often misses, with a player base that is predominantly female (over 60%).

Genshin Impact VS Candy Crush Saga

There is no single "best." The data shows a split. Genshin Impact is the "best" technical and immersive achievement, proving that complex, high-fidelity games can thrive on mobile. Candy Crush Saga isthe"best"casualphenomenon,demonstratinghowasimple, perfect loop can create a multi-decade, multi-billion dollar empire.
