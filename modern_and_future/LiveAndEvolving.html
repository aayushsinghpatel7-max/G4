Live service games and Evolving worlds

A live service game, often categorized under the broader term Games as a Service, is a type of video game structured around continuous content delivery and monetization. This approach alters how players interact with games, so instead of playing through a fixed story line and forgetting about the game afterwards, players are encouraged to return regularly to participate in events or to unlock limited time awards.

Key characteristics:

  • Regular Content Updates: This may include new levels, items, story arcs, characters, or gameplay modes. Updates are often have themes and timed around seasons or chapters.
  • Persistent Online Connectivity: Most live service games require a constant internet connection, even for single-player content.
  • Monetization Beyond the Initial Sale: Rather than relying solely on unit sales(although many are free to play), these games generate revenue through microtransactions, in-game currencies, cosmetics, battle passes, and sometimes subscriptions.

Notable examples

These are some of the most successful live service games:

  • Fortnite: Updated biweekly with new cosmetics, map changes and collaborative events.
  • Destiny 2: Features rolling expansions and seasons that introduce narrative arcs and activities.
  • World of Warcraft and League of legends: These are long standing examples of live service games that depend on an evolving world to keep players engaged and to provide continuous action.

The first Live Service game

One of the first mainstream live service games was RuneScape which was launched in 2001. It was a browser based, free to play and supported with regular content updates. A similar case was World of Warcraft, which used paid expansions and a subscription model to support continuous updates. These games provided the basic concept: persistent online experiences increase both engagement and revenue.

The genre defining moment was in 2017 with the success of Fortnite: Battle Royal, its seasonal model, with the regular balance changes and an ever evolving in-game world. This became the blueprint for future live service games, Aswell as the use of a free to play model with cosmetic monetization was especially influential.
