The Beginning of Super Mario

Super Mario first appeared in 1981 in the arcade game Donkey Kong, where he was known as “Jumpman.” He was created by Shigeru Miyamoto for Nintendo. The character quickly became popular because of his simple design and fun gameplay. Later, Nintendo decided to give him his own game and his famous name, Mario.

The Rise of a Legend

In 1985, Super Mario Bros. was released for the Nintendo Entertainment System (NES). The game became a huge success all over the world. Players loved exploring colorful worlds, jumping on enemies, and rescuing Princess Peach from Bowser. It changed video games forever and made Mario the most famous video game character in history.

Evolution Through the Years

Over the years, Mario appeared in many different games — from Super Mario World to Super Mario 64, and later Super Mario Odyssey. Each game added new ideas, better graphics, and exciting adventures. Mario also became a racer, a tennis player, and even a doctor in other Nintendo games.
