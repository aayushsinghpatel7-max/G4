Playstation icons

The original PlayStation (PSX) didn't just win the 5th console generation; it redefined gaming as a mainstream, adult-facing hobby. Sony's strategy was built on two pillars: the massive storage capacity of the CD-ROM and a revolutionary marketing campaign that treated games as a cool, cinematic art form.

Final Fantasy VII (1997)

This was the game that brought epic-scale Japanese RPGs to the West. Previously a niche genre, FFVII's 3-disc adventure, combined with its groundbreaking use of pre-rendered backgrounds and cinematic Full-Motion Video (FMV) cutscenes, made it a global phenomenon.

Key Impact:

  • Brought the JRPG genre to the mainstream Western audience.
  • Pioneered the use of expensive CGI cutscenes (FMVs) in gameplay.
  • Its success was a major factor in the PlayStation's victory over the N64.

Metal Gear Solid (1998)

Hideo Kojima's masterpiece defined a new genre: "Tactical Espionage Action." MGS felt more like an interactive Hollywood blockbuster than a simple game, pioneering complex, adult-oriented plots, extensive voice acting, and fourth-wall-breaking moments that are still discussed today.

Breaking the Fourth Wall: The game famously required the player to look on the back of the physical CD case to find a character's Codec frequency.

The Other Pillars

Tomb Raider (1996)

Introduced the world to Lara Croft, arguably the first true 3D video game icon. It defined 3D action-adventure gameplay, combining exploration, puzzles, and platforming in fully realized 3D environments.

Resident Evil (1996)

Capcom coined the term "survival horror" with this game. It masterfully used pre-rendered backgrounds and fixed camera angles (similar to horror films) to build tension and mask the console's limitations.
